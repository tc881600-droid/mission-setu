The Dual Challenge

MODELLING

Indian metropolises like Bengaluru are expanding faster than legacy analytical methods can track. Satellite road extraction must overcome spectral blindness and deliver actionable topology for planners.

Stagnation

Legacy analytical methods cannot keep pace with rapidly expanding cities, limiting actionable insight for urban planners.

09How We Measure Success
Evaluation Parameters

HOW WE MEASURE
SUCCESS

Five rigorously chosen metrics cover segmentation quality, graph fidelity, and real-world generalisation.

IoU & Dice Score

Segmentation accuracy with specific focus on Occlusion-Recall — the recovery of roads under shadows and canopy.

Generalisation

Success rate across diverse terrains: dense urban, forested suburban, and rural landscapes.

Connectivity Ratio

Percentage increase in the largest connected component after the MST healing phase.

Topological Accuracy

Compare the final graph against OSM benchmarks via Average Path Length error between random point pairs, ground-truth vs. model graph.

Length-Complete / Relaxed IoU

A 3–5 pixel tolerance buffer: predicted road pixels within the buffer of ground truth count as true positives, avoiding penalties for minor alignment shifts.
